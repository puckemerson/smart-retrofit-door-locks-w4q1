## My Experience with the Lockly Vision Elite

I've been using the Lockly Vision Elite for several months now, and it represents an interesting convergence of two smart home categories: the retrofit smart lock and the video doorbell. The core premise here is that by combining both functions into a single device, you get visual verification of who's at your door before you grant access remotely.

The security approach impressed me more than I expected. The PIN pad uses a shuffling number pattern that changes with each use, which theoretically prevents someone from observing which keys show wear or oil from repeated touches. I've found the fingerprint reader responsive, though like most biometric systems, it occasionally requires a second attempt when my fingers are particularly dry or wet. The ability to register multiple fingerprints and create temporary PIN codes for guests has proven genuinely useful.

Installation was straightforward but not trivial. Because this is a retrofit design that works with your existing deadbolt, I didn't need to replace my entire lock mechanism. The exterior unit mounts over the existing hardware, while the interior component replaces the thumb turn. This took me about 45 minutes, which included some trial and error with alignment. I'd recommend carefully checking compatibility with your specific door thickness and deadbolt type before purchasing.

The integrated video doorbell functionality adds real value to the package. Being able to see who's requesting entry before I unlock the door remotely addresses a security concern I had with standard smart locks. The camera provides adequate resolution for identifying visitors, and the two-way audio works well enough for brief conversations. Motion detection triggers alerts to my phone, though I did need to adjust sensitivity settings to reduce false alerts from passing cars.

Connectivity has been mostly reliable through my home WiFi network, though I did experience occasional delays when my internet connection was under heavy load. The app interface handles both lock control and video feed access competently, if not elegantly. I can review access logs to see when the door was unlocked and by which method, which provides useful accountability.

Battery life has been acceptable, though the video component does drain power faster than locks without cameras. I've needed to recharge roughly every two to three months with moderate use.

The main limitation is price. This sits at the premium end of smart lock options, and you're paying for the convenience of an integrated solution. If you already have a video doorbell you're satisfied with, a standard smart lock would be more economical. But for someone starting fresh or replacing both devices simultaneously, the Vision Elite makes practical sense.

Overall, I find the Lockly Vision Elite to be a well-executed product that successfully integrates two useful functions. It's not perfect, and the premium pricing requires consideration, but the security features and visual verification capability have made me feel more confident about remote access management.
