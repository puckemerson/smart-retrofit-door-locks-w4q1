## My Experience with the Lockly Secure Plus

I installed the Lockly Secure Plus on my front door as part of my ongoing effort to modernize my home security without replacing existing hardware. This smart lock sits in the middle ground between basic electronic locks and high-end smart security systems, offering a blend of biometric access, traditional PIN entry, and remote connectivity.

## Security Approach

The standout feature is the fingerprint sensor combined with what Lockly calls anti-peep technology on the keypad. Each time I approach the lock, the numbers display in a different pattern on the touchscreen, which theoretically prevents someone from identifying my PIN by watching which physical locations I touch. In practice, this adds a second of cognitive load as I locate the numbers, but I've grown accustomed to it.

The fingerprint scanner works reliably once properly enrolled. I added multiple fingers during setup, which proved wise since recognition occasionally fails on the first attempt, particularly when my hands are cold or wet. The lock stores biometric data locally rather than in the cloud, which addresses one concern about remote data breaches.

## Installation Process

The retrofit design meant I didn't need to replace my deadbolt or make any irreversible changes to my door. The installation took me roughly 30 minutes with a screwdriver and the included template. I did need to make minor adjustments to get the interior and exterior components properly aligned, and the instructions could have been clearer on this point. The battery compartment is accessible from inside without removing the entire unit, which is a practical design choice.

## Connectivity and App Performance

The companion app provides remote locking, unlock notifications, and activity logs. I can see who entered and when, which gives me useful oversight. However, the app occasionally loses connection, requiring me to close and reopen it. The interface feels functional rather than refined, lacking the polish I've experienced with some other smart home products.

Integration with voice assistants is possible, though setup involved more steps than I expected. Once configured, I can check lock status through voice commands, but I've chosen not to enable voice unlocking for security reasons.

## Daily Use Realities

After several months of use, the lock has become part of my routine. The fingerprint access works well enough that I rarely use the PIN pad anymore. Battery life has been acceptable, though I keep backup batteries on hand. I've needed to restart the lock twice due to firmware-related freezes, which was inconvenient but resolved quickly.

The build quality feels adequate rather than exceptional. The materials are solid plastic and metal, but don't convey the same premium feel as locks costing significantly more. For the price point, this seems reasonable.

## Overall Assessment

The Lockly Secure Plus delivers on its core promise of adding smart functionality to an existing door setup without major modifications. The security features are thoughtful, and daily operation is generally smooth. The app and connectivity aspects feel like they could use refinement, and I'd appreciate more consistent performance. For someone seeking a middle-tier smart lock with biometric access and reasonable security features, this represents a viable option, though not without minor compromises.
